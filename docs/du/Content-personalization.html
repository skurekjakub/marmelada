    Content personalization

    Personalization allows you to custom-tailor the content for your website visitors. You can personalize what your visitors see based on their previous interactions. You can vary the content of emails that recipients receive in their inboxes based on their preferences. You can also save tedious work and define who receives which content with marketing automation or using 3rd party Artificial Intelligence or Machine Learning services.

    In their 2019 study, The Relevancy Group and Liveclicker reported that marketers who used their customer behavior and purchase data to create advanced personalization scenarios earned up to $20 on each $1 link.

      Personalization in Kentico Xperience

      Marketing communication should never be a "one-size-fits-all" interaction. With the correct content served at the right time, you can turn your visitors into loyal followers of your brands.

      Kentico Xperience helps you collect customer data, analyze it, and custom-fit the user experience across all future interactions. You can offer different website content to new users and to your existing customers. You can send alternative emails to different customers, all within one email marketing campaign. Or you can personalize any message using information about your customers' purchases.

      First, let's create a personalization variant of a widget.

      1. In the Pages application, navigate to the Landing pages -> Coffee samples page.

      2. Click the Personalize (ICON) icon in the header of the Hero image widget.

      3. Select the Is in contact group condition type to create a new personalization variant of the widget.

      4. From the list of contact groups, select the Interested in coffee samples.

      5. Enter a variant name, e.g., Variant for Interested in coffee samples and click the Add variant button.

      Let's edit the content of the personalized widget variant and change the background image.

      1. Hover your mouse over the Hero image widget to display widget's inline properties.

      2. In the media dialog, click the Browse button and select the banner2.png media file to change the widget's background.

      3. Click Save to include your changes.
